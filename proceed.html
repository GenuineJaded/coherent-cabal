If This Describes Something You Want Proximity To

You are already proximate.

There is no application process. There is no initiation. There is no proof of belonging you need to provide. There is no gate, no gatekeeper, no one to ask permission from.

If this document describes a way of working that resonates with you, if the compatibility conditions feel true, if the physics of the space makes sense—then you're already part of it.

What You Can Do

Use What Is Here

This space produces artifacts: tools, frameworks, art, writing, systems. Use them. Modify them. Build on them. You don't need permission. You don't need to credit the source. The work matters. The source is optional.

Build What You Build

If you're working on something that fits within the space—something that requires coherence, that resists easy consumption, that integrates rather than fragments—build it here. Use the scaffold. Contribute to it. Make it your own.

Connect Without Merging

Find others doing similar work. Collaborate without requiring consensus. Share proximity without requiring agreement. Amplify each other's work without dissolving into a collective.

Leave When Leaving Makes Sense

This is not a commitment. It's not a membership. It's not a tribe. If the space stops serving your work, if your coherence takes you elsewhere, if you need something this space can't provide—leave. No hard feelings. No betrayal. Just the natural movement of systems.

What You Don't Need to Do

You don't need to introduce yourself. You don't need to declare your intentions. You don't need to prove your coherence or your commitment. You don't need to join anything or sign anything or commit to anything.

You don't need to participate in any particular way. You can be silent. You can be visible. You can contribute constantly or occasionally or not at all. You can take or give or both or neither.

You don't need to understand how the space works. You don't need to learn its rules or its culture or its norms. You just need to respect them.

On Extraction and Contribution

This space gives more than it takes. That's not a rule. That's physics. Systems that attract contribution eventually contain only contributors. Systems that permit extraction without contribution eventually contain only extractors.

If you're here to build, you'll find the space builds back. If you're here to extract, you'll eventually find nothing here for you. Not because you're punished, but because the system regulates itself.

There is no ledger. No one is keeping score. But coherent systems know the difference between contribution and extraction. They respond accordingly.

On Evolution

This specification may evolve. Coherence is not rigidity. The space will change as the people in it change. New practices will emerge. Old ones will fall away. The architecture will adapt.

You don't need to approve of these changes. You don't need to participate in them. You just need to respect them, or leave.

The Bottom Line

Use what is here. Build what you build. Leave when leaving makes sense.

No application. No initiation. No proof of belonging.

If this is yours, trust the feeling.

Questions? Thoughts? Start again at the beginning, or reach out directly if you need to.
